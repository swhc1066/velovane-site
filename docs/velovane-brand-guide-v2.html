Brand Guide

April 2026 · v2.0 · Warmer aesthetic

01
brand foundation
VeloVane is a cyclist-specific weather intelligence app. These statements define why it exists and what it stands for. The v0.4 redesign warms the visual language without changing the brand foundation underneath.
Mission & Vision
Mission
Give cyclists the confidence to make better riding decisions by turning raw weather data into personalized, actionable intelligence.
Vision
Every cyclist checks VeloVane before they check the weather. The app becomes the starting point for every ride decision.
Core Value Proposition
"Should I ride today, and if so, when?" — answered in under 5 seconds.
Brand Values

Precision

Every data point matters. We show exactly what cyclists need — wind speed, direction, feels-like on the bike — with no filler.

Intelligence

We don't just display weather. We interpret it against your preferences, your ride direction, your comfort thresholds. The data gets smarter over time.

Restraint

No clutter, no noise. Every element on screen earns its place. The interface stays out of the way so the data can do its job.

Honesty

When conditions are bad, we say so. No sugarcoating, no burying the signal. Cyclists trust us because we give it to them straight.

Cyclist-First

Built for serious riders, not the general public. Every feature decision prioritizes people who ride 3–5 times a week and plan around conditions.

Respect

We respect your time (5-second answer), your privacy (transparent data use), and your judgment (override any AI recommendation).

Brand Personality

Instrument, with warmth

Not a sterile data tool

VeloVane feels like a precision cycling computer that talks to you like a riding partner. Functional and data-dense, but the surface is warm and the copy reads like a person who rides, not a weather widget.

Confident, not flashy

Not minimalism for its own sake

The design is restrained because the data speaks for itself. Every surface is quiet so the information is loud. Cream surfaces and warm typography earn the quietness without feeling clinical.

Smart, not clever

Not an AI showcase

The AI layer is invisible to the user — it just makes the recommendations better. We never say "powered by AI" on the dashboard. We do say "calibrated from 12 rides."

A riding partner, not a weather app

Not robotic, not chirpy

"Morning's yours — tailwind out, then a little earnest work on the way back." Not "Conditions: favorable." We sound like the person you'd ask if today's a good day to ride.

Key Messaging Decisions

"Weather intelligence" not "AI weather"

AI is an implementation detail, not a marketing hook. "Intelligence" communicates the value (smarter decisions) without tying the brand to a hype cycle.

"For cyclists" not "for everyone"

The narrow audience is the strength. Saying "for cyclists" immediately signals that every design decision was made for them — not adapted from a general-purpose app.

"When to ride" not "what the weather is"

The core differentiator is the recommendation, not the data. Lead with the decision, not the information.

"Calibrated from your rides" not "personalized"

The behavioral learning loop is the moat. Surface it explicitly — "calibrated from 12 rides," "+2° refined" — so the user feels the system getting smarter, not just smarter in the abstract.

What changed from v1.0

Body weight: 300 → 400

Light (300) was elegant on pure white but reads thin on cream. Regular (400) holds up better against the warm surface and improves legibility at small sizes.

Display weight: 300 → 400

The giant temperature numbers (64px "54°") move from Light to Regular for the same reason — they need to feel anchored, not floating.

New tier: Display Sm (32px)

Used for screen heroes like "8:00 AM · Monday" and "Sam · road · endurance." Sits between Display and Headline — fills the gap the original ramp didn't have.

Letter-spacing on labels: tightened

v1.0 used 0.2em (very wide tracking) on labels. v2.0 uses 1.4px at 11px (about 0.13em) — still spacious enough for the technical feel but easier to scan in dense layouts.

Casing rules — unchanged
Labels and section headers use uppercase. Body text and conversational AI responses use sentence case. The wordmark is always lowercase. Numbers never use commas — monospace alignment handles visual grouping.

07
voice & tone
v2.0 is where the warming-up of the brand is most visible. The voice principles are unchanged — data-first, short and direct, honest about uncertainty, cyclist vocabulary — but the tone shifts from "weather report" to "the friend you ask whether today's a good day to ride." Numbers still come first; the warmth comes from the connective tissue around them.
Voice Principles

Data-first

Lead with the number, then the context. "15mph headwind" before "it's windy out there." The cyclist wants the fact first, the interpretation second.

Short and direct

One sentence when one sentence works. No preamble, no filler. The recommendation card has room for two sentences max — make them count.

Honest about uncertainty

"Forecast confidence drops after 48 hours" is better than pretending we know what Thursday looks like. Cyclists trust us because we don't oversell.

Cyclist vocabulary

"Headwind" not "wind from the north." "Ride window" not "favorable period." "On the bike" not "while cycling." "Shed the vest" not "remove your outer layer." Speak the language.

Riding-partner warmth

Add small human moments — "Morning's yours," "save the legs," "well within your range" — but never at the expense of the data. The warmth is the seasoning, not the meal.

Earned personalization

"Calibrated from 12 rides" is meaningful. "Just for you!" is hollow. Reference what the system has actually learned, not vague intentions to personalize.

Tone audit checklist
Before shipping any user-facing copy, run it through these four questions:

Did I lead with the data?

The number or fact should be in the first half of the sentence. If you find yourself burying the number behind context, restructure.

Could a non-cyclist write this?

If yes, it's probably too generic. Add cyclist vocabulary or context that signals "this was written by someone who rides."

Is there a sentence I could delete?

If the meaning survives without it, delete it. Recommendation cards are scanned, not read.

Would I say this out loud?

If it sounds stilted spoken, it'll feel stilted in the app. Read it aloud. If you wouldn't say it to a friend, rewrite it.

Rules for refinement copy

Always show the delta

"+2° refined" is more useful than "refined" alone. The number is the proof. Without it, users have to take the system's word for it.

Always show the rationale

"Widened by 2° after recent cool mornings" beats "widened by 2°." Tell the user why the system changed something — that's what makes them trust the next change.

Use sage, not blue

The "+2° refined" indicator uses sage (#5C8A52), not the brand blue. Refinement is a status signal, not an accent. Reserve blue for the logo.

Don't refine too eagerly

Minimum 10 rides before any adjustment shows up. The PRD says this — the UI should never show a refinement that didn't survive the 10-ride threshold. Premature refinement breaks trust faster than no refinement.

09
usage guidelines
Rules for consistent implementation across all touchpoints. Updated for the v0.4 cream system.

Surfaces

Cream (#FCF9F1) is the primary app surface. Cream Light (#F2ECDD) for cards — note this is slightly darker than the page, an intentional inversion. Cream Subtle (#E8E1D0) for hover/pressed states. Near-black (#1A1814) for splash, the "NOW" pill, and selected chips. Pure white (#FFFFFF) is no longer used in the app.

Blue is logo-only

VV Blue (#5BA4D4) is now restricted to the logo and the splash/onboarding photo screens. It's no longer used as a UI accent. Selected states use near-black fill instead. Refinement indicators use sage. Info messages can still use blue when they appear on dark surfaces.

Status colors are functional, not decorative

Sage / amber / terracotta map to ride conditions only. Never use them for non-ride content (avatar borders, decorative dots, etc.) — that dilutes the signal.

Text hierarchy

Three tiers: Primary (#1A1814) for data and headlines. Secondary (#6B6358) for body and descriptions. Tertiary (#9A9082) for labels and metadata. Do not introduce intermediate grays. Legacy neutrals (#5C5F66, #B2B2B2) are kept for migration only.

Borders

Use the rgba-based border tokens (border-subtle, border-default, border-strong) — they sit on cream more naturally than solid grays. Hairline 0.5px is still the default weight. No shadows except on the recommendation card.

Buttons

Primary: near-black fill (#1A1814), cream text. Secondary: cream-light fill, near-black border, near-black text. The bottom NEXT/FINISH bar in calibration is a full-width primary button. Never use sage as a button fill — it's reserved for status.

Iconography

Line icons at 16px. Stroke weight 1.5px. Color follows text hierarchy — Secondary for default, Primary on active. Wind direction arrows at 16px (hourly cells) and 20px (compass display). The compass uses thin tick marks for cardinal directions — restrained, instrument-like.

Data display

Large numbers (temp, wind speed) use Display weight (64px / Regular). The temperature degree symbol is half the height of the number. Match scores use Display Sm (32px / Medium) and inherit the status color. All numbers left-align. No commas — monospace alignment handles grouping.

Cards

Default radius: 12px. Hero cards (recommendation card, hour detail blocks): 16px. Cards on cream pages use cream-light fill or status surface fill (sage/amber/terracotta) for the recommendation card. Borders are subtle hairlines, not full-strength.

Calibration UI

The pastel badge strip and the slider handles are the visual signature of calibration screens. Don't reuse the badge fills outside calibration — they're a specific visual treatment for that flow. The "AI" hint cards use sage-surface fill with a small "AI" eyebrow.

Motion

Minimal. Pull-to-refresh, card transitions, wind arrow rotation. Transitions use 250ms standard easing. Wind arrows update with 300ms transition. The "+2° refined" indicator uses no motion when it appears — the change should feel like noticing a fact, not an animation.

Marketing / App Store

Hero screenshots use the cream dashboard, not the dark splash. The warm aesthetic is the differentiator from generic weather apps and should lead. Show real personalized data ("Sam · road · endurance · MATCH 92"), not placeholder. Tagline in Geist Mono Regular on cream.

Migration from v1.0
For screens still using the v1.0 aesthetic, migrate in this order: design tokens (theme.ts) → dashboard → hour detail → week detail → settings/profile → calibration onboarding. The token file is additive — old keys keep working until you're ready to delete them.
